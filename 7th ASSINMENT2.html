Information of Sport

1.Surfing- Surfing is a water sport done in the ocean or sea. The surfer uses their surfboard to catch a wave and ride in towards the shore. It has become a popular sport among both men and women of all ages. With lifestyles and regimens freer than those of most sports, surfers comprise a unique culture.

2.Sailing- The sport of sailing involves a variety of competitive sailing formats that are sanctioned through various sailing federations and yacht clubs. Racing disciplines include matches within a fleet of sailing craft, between a pair thereof or among teams. We keep Saling sport for the practice of the person of Olympic racers or persons who want to learn.

3.Swimming- Swimming is an individual or team racing sport that requires the use of one's entire body to move through water. Competitive swimming is one of the most popular Olympic sports, with varied distance events in butterfly, backstroke, breaststroke, freestyle, and individual medley.

4.Wind Surfing- Wind surfing is a surface water sport that is a combination of surfing and sailing. It is also referred to as "sailboarding" and "boardsailing", and emerged in the early 1970s from the surf culture of California. The sport has two distinct interest groups, namely Racing and Riding, with many participants embracing both.

5.Jet Skiing- Jet Skiiking racing is a water sport similar to powerboat racing, in which riders use jet skis to compete. The sport is mostly practiced at a recreational level in several countries around the world. The course used for races are created with buoys and are usually oval in shape.

6.Wake Boarding- Wake boarding is a water sport that involves riding a wakeboard over the surface of a body of water. This sport, that appeared in the beginning of the eighties, has been inspired by surfing, water skiing and snowboarding.
